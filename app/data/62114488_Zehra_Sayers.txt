Zehra Sayers (born 25 September 1953) is a German structural biologist. She formerly held Turkish and British citizenship but relinquished these in 2021 to obtain German citizenship. == Early life and education == Sayers was born in Turkey. She studied physics at Boğaziçi University in Istanbul. For her postgraduate studies Sayers moved to the United Kingdom. She obtained British citizenship in consequence of her first marriage to British geoscientist Colin Sayers. Whilst working in Germany she obtained her habilitation in 1996 with a thesis from the University of Hamburg, where her second husband, Turkish- American foreign policy expert Ahmet Evin, served as Professor until 1995. == Career == In 1998 Sayers returned to Turkey, joining the faculty of Sabancı University as the partner of Ahmet Evin. Established by the Turkish industrial and financial conglomerate Hacı Ömer Sabancı Holding A.Ş., Sabancı University was the first and only university where Sayers served as faculty member; she remained at the institution until her retirement in 2022. Sayers has not published any monographs; in 2021, the year she obtained German citizenship, she drew attention with an article on Turkish politics, published by EuroScience, in which she described the Turkish Armed Forces as one of the "established fundamental pillars in the state" and took Turkey's ruling Justice and Development Party to task for having "systematically steered the country away from its secular constitution", imposed by the Turkish Armed Forces following the 1980 Turkish coup d'état. == References == Category:Living people Category:1953 births Category:Boğaziçi University alumni Category:Academic staff of Sabancı University Category:Academics of the Open University Category:Structural biologists Category:Women biologists
