ζ Pictoris, Latinised as Zeta Pictoris, is a solitary star in the southern constellation of Pictor. It is visible to the naked eye with an apparent visual magnitude of +5.43. Based upon an annual parallax shift of 28.00 mas as seen from the Earth, the system is located 116.5 light years from the Sun. This is an evolving F-type subgiant star with a stellar classification of F6 IV. It is a thin disk star with an estimated 1.4 times the mass of the Sun and about 5.3 times the Sun's radius.
