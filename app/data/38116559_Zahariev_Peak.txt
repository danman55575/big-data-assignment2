thumb|Location of Oscar II Coast on Antarctic Peninsula. Zahariev Peak (, ) is a rocky, partly ice-free peak rising to 816 mReference Elevation Model of Antarctica. Polar Geospatial Center. University of Minnesota, 2019 in Metlichina Ridge on Oscar II Coast, Graham Land in Antarctica. It overlooks Punchbowl Glacier to the northeast and Borima Bay to the southwest. The feature is named after the Bulgarian meteorologist Vasil Zahariev (1929-2006) who worked at the Soviet base Mirny in 1967–69. ==Location== Zahariev Peak is located at , which is 3.9 km south of St. Angelariy Peak, 2.58 km northwest of Chapanov Peak and 5.45 km east-northeast of Yordanov Nunatak.
