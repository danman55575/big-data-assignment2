Zenobia pulverulenta, the honeycup, is a North American species of shrubs, in the genus Zenobia, in the family Ericaceae.Discover Life, honeycup, Zenobia pulverulenta It is native to coastal plain of the Southeastern United States, in North Carolina, South Carolina, and Virginia.Biota of North America Program 2014 county distribution mapPollard, Charles Louis. 1895. Bulletin of the Torrey Botanical Club 22(5): 232 ==Description== Zenobia pulverulenta is a deciduous or semi-evergreen shrub growing to 0.5-1.8 m tall. The leaves are spirally arranged, ovate to elliptic, long.Flora of North America, Zenobia pulverulenta (Bartram ex Willdenow) Pollard, 1895. Honey-cup The flowers are white, bell-shaped, long and broad, and sweetly scented.
