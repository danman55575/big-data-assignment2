thumb|400px|Zerubbabel Before Darius by Nikolaus Knüpfer Zerubbabel Before Darius is an oil on oak panel painting by Nikolaus Knüpfer, now in the Hermitage Museum, in Saint Petersburg (inv. GE-6980). It was dated to after 1644 by Jo Saxton,Jo Saxton, Nicolaus Knupfer, an original artist: Monograph and catalogue raisonné of paintings and drawings. — Doornspijk, 2005. — page 16. a date also accepted by the work's owner. The work is signed at the foot of the throne on the left hand side. The frame is significantly damaged and the bottom right of the panel is chipped away. It illustrates the 'Darius contest', an episode in 1 Esdras (3:4 to 4:63) in which Darius's bodyguards debated what was the strongest thing in existence. One spoke argued it to be wine and another that it was the king, but Darius judged the competition won by Zerubbabel, who argued "Women are strongest, but above all things truth is victor". Очерк-путеводитель. — Л., Искусство, 1988. — p. 147 It has previously been mistitled The Story of Artaxerxes, Daniel Defending Susanna from Slander and Allegory of Justice, with Kuznetsov restoring the present title in 1974, Yuri Ivanovich Kuznetsov, Nikolaus Knupfer (1603? —1655) // Oud Holland. - 1974. - No. 88 (3). - P. 169-181. though Dutch writers now referring to it as Zerubbabel Asking Darius's Permission to Restore the Temple in Jerusalem, referring to the reward granted to Zerubbabel by Darius for winning the debate (4:42-63). It first appears in the written record on 10 May 1764, when it was bought by privy counsellor Broggia in Bonn at a posthumous auction of Clemens August of Bavaria's collection. It was acquired by Paul I of Russia at the end of the 18th century - on the reverse is Paul's red wax seal and a label inscribed "ПДМ 1919", referring to the Pavlovsk Palace Museum, where it was kept.
