Zeytin-Kosh is a mountain in Crimea and the third highest mountain in the Crimean Mountains. Its height stands 1537 meters above sea level. ==Etymology== The name of the mountain is of Crimean Tatar origin and literally means "olive kosh" (zeytün or in the southern coast dialect zeytin - "olive, olive tree"; qoş - "koshara, sheepfold"). ==Notes== == References == Category:Crimean Mountains Category:One-thousanders of Ukraine
