The Zay (; ) is a river in Russia, a left-bank tributary of the Kama. It is long, and its drainage basin covers .«Река Ульвич», Russian State Water Registry It begins near Mikhaylovka, Leninogorsky District, Tatarstan and falls to the Kama south-west of Nizhnekamsk. At its headwaters the river is named the Steppe Zay (, Stepnoy Zay, , Dala Zäye). The lower reach of the river after the confluence with Forest Zay River, , is named simply Zay. Also, one of headstream Steppe Zay's tributaries is also named Zay. Other major tributaries are the Moshkara, Zay-Karatay, Ursala, Aktashka, and Shumyshka rivers. There are two reservoirs, Zainsk and Karabash Reservoirs constructed in Zay valley. Since 1978 it is protected as a natural monument of Tatarstan.
